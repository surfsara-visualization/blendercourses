Description

In this course you will learn the basics of the Python API in Blender. You will learn how the API is structured and eventually start making some simple geometry with code. Another important part of the course is that you will work on a visualisation based on your own research or other endeavours.

The course is set up to run over two weeks, beginning with an introductory webinar. During the course period you work on the theory and exercises in your own time using the tutorials and video lectures. We provide a communication channel with the course teachers that you can use for questions and feedback during the course. There are also several webinars where you present your progress and we discuss your project and make sure you are able to make progress. Part of the course material, like our videos and tutorials, is also available outside the course timetable.

Your own project and data

To get the most out of the course we would like you to take a dataset from your work that you want to use for visualisation with Blender. This course will give you the skills to start on your own project. During the interactive moments (webinars and chat channel) our visualisation team will help you further with your specific project and challenges. So please collect some data you think would be great to make a 3D visualisation of. During the webinars we will expect input from you also. We would like you to present your visualisation goals, your data and your first try at making a visualisation with Blender.

This course is a pilot

Due to the Covid-19 situation we have decided to start an online course, as alternative to our regular on-site courses. This course module is intentionally small because we want to test how and see if we can effectively use online tools for our training programs, especially for a hands-on topic like using Blender. We hope that you are open to giving us feedback on the setup and execution of this course. The next parts of the course will be improved based on your input.

Prerequisites

  • Basic knowledge of Blender: getting around the interface, making and adding geometry, some basics on materials
  • Basic knowledge of Python
  • An idea and/or some data for your own project to work on during the course.

What you need

  • Computer with Blender installed
  • Blender version 2.83 LTS
  • Internet connection to join us on Discord
  • Account with Discord to join the chat and webinar server

The webinars

What we will do during the webinars

During the webinars we will not explain theory, but they will be interactive moments during the course for you to get feedback on your work. They will be the moment for you to ask questions to our team. Questions about the theory and exercises in the course, but also the moment for you to discuss what you want to do with Blender and how best to do this. Thus, the more you prepare for the webinar the more you can get out of them!

Prepare for the webinars

During the introduction webinar we hope you grace us with a brief introduction of who you are, why you wanted to do this course and what you would like to get out of it. Maybe you have a project for which you want to make a visualisation with Blender?

For the feedback webinars please prepare a short 5 min presentation telling us about your project and what you tried to visualise with Blender and how. It will be a great opportunity to make progress!

How to join the webinar

For the introduction webinar we will test Discord to see if it is capable of hosting a webinar. Please make sure you installed Discord, joined our server and requested to join the webinar channel. See instructions on installing Discord here.

Discord

We use a Discord server for text, audio and video communication. It also allows screen sharing to help you out where needed.

To get started download the Discord application and create an account. Note that the browser-based version of Discord does not always support streaming your screen/video, plus we experienced some audio issues with that version, hence we suggest to use the standalone application.

In Discord join our server through https://discord.gg/mVjYzbm and introduce yourself in the #general text channel so we can invite you into the course channels.

Some more information on how to use Discord, see this and this.

Where to start

Start with reading the tutorials and watching the videos linked within. Also do the exercises in the tutorial and try to reproduce all that is done in the tutorial and the videos. When you are done with that, start your own project as described in the last chapter of the tutorials.

One day before a Feedback webinar (check the time table), think about how you want to present your project to the teachers and the other students during the webinar and prepare some material to share. What are you trying to do in your project? What works what does not work? Maybe have a screenshot or a screenshare of you visualisation ready.

If you are stuck or do not know how to continue, drop us a line on the Discord server. Or wait for one of the webinars to ask a questions.
