Course: Blender for Scientific visualisations

Part 1: Introduction to Python Scripting in Blender

8? JUNE 2020, duration of 2 weeks, Register here (link coming)

Description

In this course you will learn the basic foundations of the Python API in Blender. You will learn how the API is structured and eventually start making some simple geometry with code. Another important part of the course is that you will work on a visualisation based on your own research or other endeavours. Part of the course material, like our youtube videos and tutorials, are available outside the course timetable. Within the timetable and for the registered participants we also offer two webinars and a chat service. During the webinars we discus your projects and make sure you are able to make progres. During the course registered participants can also use our chat server for questions and feedback when they are stuck.

Your own project

To get the most out of the course we would like you to take a dataset from your work that you want tot try and visualise with Blender. This course will give you the first skills to start plan and work on your own project. In the interactive moments (webinars and chat channels) our visualisation team will help you further with your specific project. So please collect some data you think would be great to make a 3D visualisation of. During the webinars we will expect input from you also. We would like you to present your visualisation goals, your data and your first try making a visualisation with Blender.

This course is a pilot

This course is intentially small because we want to test how and see if we coud use more online and distance tools for our education programs. We hope that you are open for giving us feedback on the setup and execution of this course. The next part of the course will be rolled using your input.

The webinars will be interactive moments during the course. They will be the moment for you to ask questions to our team. Questions about the theory and exercises in the course, but also the moment for you to discuss what you want to do with Blender and how to do this.

Prerequisites

  • Basic knowledge of Blender: getting around the interface, making and adding geometry, some basics on shaders
  • Basic knowledge of Python
  • An idea and maybe some data for your own project to work on during the course.

What you need

  • Computer with Blender installed and working
  • Blender version 2.82a
  • Internet connection to join the webinars
  • Account with discord to join the chat server

About

SURF (collaborative organisation for compute in Dutch education and research) offers courses on several topics within the PRACE Partnership. This website accompanies courses from the Team Visualisation focussed on using Blender, the free and open source 3D creation suite for scientific goals.
